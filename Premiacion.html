    Alarm System and Vital Signs Indicator with Artificial Vision and Brain Signals.

    UCB Neurotech at FUNTEC 2023

    UCB Neurotech achieved outstanding success by participating in FUNTEC 2023. with the project "Alarm System and Vital Signs Indicator with Artificial Vision and Brain Signals". At this science and technology fair, which took place at the Bolivian Catholic University "San Pablo," the team managed to obtain second place in the category of student societies.

    The project's primary objective is to improve the well-being and safety of quadriplegics in their homes by developing a home automation system using a Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) and SSVEP (Steady State Visually Evoked Potential) signals. Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) and SSVEP (Steady State Visually Evoked Potential) signals. Specific objectives include the acquisition of alpha waves through electroencephalography for emergency detection, the capture of SSVEP (Steady State Visually Evoked Potential) signals and the capture of SSVEP signals for home automation control, the selection of suitable sensors for the home automation prototype and the control prototype, the implementation of presence detection through artificial vision, the monitoring of vital signs for the supervision of vital signs for user health monitoring, the development of voice command functionalities, and the implementation of remote control via the Telegram application. This project primarily seeks to improve the quality of life of people with limited mobility who face daily challenges in terms of health and safety. The lack of domotic systems in Bolivia exacerbates these problems, limiting access to medical care and making it difficult to respond to emergency situations. Inspired by the EVISS system, which integrates security sensors, health sensors, and a BCI to detect panic signals and allow for control via SSVEP, the project focuses on providing end-to-end technological solutions. The implementation of a customised Telegram bot operates the entire system, allowing people with motor disabilities to access health information, activate or deactivate the alarm, and receive alerts in emergency situations. access health information, activate or deactivate the alarm, and receive alerts in emergency situations. This approach has led to the development of a remarkable technological solution in its category, standing out in competition against other projects.

    Several tests were carried out to evaluate the performance of the project, yielding the following results, with special emphasis on the processing of alpha waves obtained by electroencephalography (EEG). These raw signals, once filtered according to their frequency range, demonstrated peak amplitudes in response to specific actions, such as intense blinks, and were used to develop an effective emergency system.

    However, due to the inter-individual variability of EEG signals, the need to customise the detection threshold for each user is emphasised. For SSVEP signals, the Principal Component Analysis (PCA) method was applied to isolate and extract essential features while reducing the dimensionality of these signals.

    It is in this way that it could be noticed that the set of elements not only obtains and processes different signals in a correct way but also emits a precise response immediately to ensure the well-being of its users.

    Thus, participation in FUNTEC 2023 represented an important milestone for UCB Neurotech, demonstrating the commitment to innovation and social impact through the application of technology in everyday life.
